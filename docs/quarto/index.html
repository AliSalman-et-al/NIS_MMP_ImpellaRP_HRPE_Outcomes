Impella RP in High-Risk Pulmonary Embolism With Cardiogenic Shock

National Inpatient Sample (NIS), 2017–2022

Author
Affiliation

Ali Salman

Dow University of Health Sciences

Study overview

This report reproduces the primary, survey-weighted analysis of Impella RP use among adults hospitalized with high-risk pulmonary embolism (PE) and cardiogenic shock in the National Inpatient Sample (NIS), 2017–2022. The primary analytic approach uses propensity score inverse probability of treatment weighting (IPTW) to address measured confounding while retaining the NIS complex survey design.

Data source and study period

We used the Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project (HCUP) National Inpatient Sample (NIS), 2017–2022, a stratified, nationally representative sample of U.S. hospital discharges. All analyses accounted for the NIS complex survey design (hospital clustering, stratification, and discharge weights).

Study population

We included discharges meeting all of the following: - Age ≥ 18 years - Pulmonary embolism present (ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes: I26.01, I26.02, I26.04, I26.09, I26.90, I26.92, I26.93, I26.94, I26.96, I26.99) - Cardiogenic shock present (ICD-10-CM: R57.0)

Exposure

Impella RP use was defined by ICD-10-PCS procedure codes 02HA3RZ or 5A0221D during the index hospitalization.

Covariates

Covariates were prespecified based on clinical relevance and availability in NIS: - Demographics: age, sex, race (collapsed category), primary payer (collapsed category), ZIP-code income quartile
- Hospital factors: region, bed size, location/teaching status
- Transfer status: transfer-in, transfer-out
- Clinical factors: right ventricular failure, left ventricular failure, Charlson comorbidity index, and year of admission

Race and payer were collapsed for analytic stability (Native American → “Other”; Self-pay and No charge → “Other”).

Outcomes

Prespecified in-hospital outcomes were: - In-hospital mortality - Major bleeding and/or transfusion (intracranial hemorrhage diagnosis codes I60–I62 and/or blood product transfusion procedures; see Appendix) - Acute kidney injury (ICD-10-CM: N17.) - Length of stay (LOS), days - Inflation-adjusted total cost, in 2022 U.S. dollars, computed from total charges using the HCUP cost-to-charge ratio and adjusted using annual Consumer Price Index ratios

Statistical analysis

We report survey-weighted descriptive statistics and compare groups by Impella RP status.

Primary approach: propensity score IPTW

Primary associations were estimated using propensity score IPTW to create a weighted pseudo-population in which measured baseline covariates were balanced between treatment groups. IPTW weights were stabilized and estimated for the average treatment effect (ATE).

To preserve national representativeness and account for the NIS complex survey design, we combined weights as:

Final analysis weight = (NIS discharge weight) × (stabilized IPTW)

We assessed covariate balance using standardized mean differences (SMDs) before and after weighting and visualized balance with a Love plot.

Outcome models (IPTW-weighted)

Outcome models were fit using the IPTW-weighted survey design: - Binary outcomes: survey-weighted generalized linear models with quasibinomial family; reported as odds ratios (OR) with 95% confidence intervals (CI) - LOS and total cost: survey-weighted Gaussian models of log(1 + outcome); exponentiated coefficients are reported as ratios (exp[β]) with 95% CI

Appendix: code groupings used to define study variables

The following code groupings were used to derive study variables from discharge diagnosis and procedure fields:

  • Pulmonary embolism: ICD-10-CM I26.01, I26.02, I26.04, I26.09, I26.90, I26.92, I26.93, I26.94, I26.96, I26.99
  • Cardiogenic shock: ICD-10-CM R57.0
  • Right ventricular failure: ICD-10-CM I50.810–I50.813 and I27.81
  • Left ventricular failure: ICD-10-CM I50.1, I50.20–I50.23, I50.30–I50.33, I50.40–I50.43
  • Impella RP: ICD-10-PCS 02HA3RZ or 5A0221D
  • Acute kidney injury: ICD-10-CM N17.
  • Major bleeding and/or transfusion: intracranial hemorrhage ICD-10-CM I60–I62 and/or blood product transfusion ICD-10-PCS procedure codes (see project code list)
